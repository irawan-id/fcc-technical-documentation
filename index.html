MAJALENGKA

Sort History

Majalengka is a district in the province of West Java , Indonesia . The capital of this district is Majalengka sub -district . This regency is 95 km northeast of Bandung City and 56 km from Cirebon City.

During the Hindu-Buddhist kingdom until the 15th century , the Majalengka Regency was divided into 3 kingdoms:

  1. The Talaga Manggung Kingdom was led by Sunan Corenda or better known as Sunan Parung
  2. Rajagaluh Kingdom was led by Prabu Cakraningrat
  3. Sindangkasih Kingdom , led by a princess named Nyi Rambut Kasih

There are many folk tales about the 3 kingdoms that are still alive today among the Majalengka people. In addition to folklore which is still believed to be there are also sites, tombs and archaeological objects, all of which in addition to being regional wealth can also be used as historical sources.

Geographical

Geographically, Majalengka Regency is located in the eastern part of West Java Province . Majalengka Regency is located at the coordinates of 108° 03'–108° 19 East Longitude, East 108° 12'–108° 25 East Longitude, North 6° 36'–5°58 South Latitude and 6° South 43'–7°44.

The northern part of this district is lowland, while the central area is hilly and the southern area is a mountainous area with the peak of Mount Ceremai bordering Kuningan Regency and Mount Cakrabuana bordering Tasikmalaya Regency and Sumedang Regency .

The northern part of this district is in the lowlands, while the southern part is in the form of mountains. Mount Ciremai (3,076 m) is located in the east, which is on the border with Kuningan Regency . This mountain is the highest mountain in West Java Province , and is a national park, under the name Gunung Ciremai National Park Geographical conditions, especially the morphology and physiography of the Majalengka Regency area, vary greatly and are influenced by differences in altitude from one area to another, with the following distribution: Lowland morphology that includes Kadipaten , Kasokandel , Panyingkiran , Dawuan , Jatiwangi , Sumberjaya , Ligung , Jatitujuh , Kertajati , Cigasong , Majalengka , Leuwimunding and Palasah districts . The slope of the land in this area is between 5%-8% with a height between 20-100 m above sea level (dpl), except in Majalengka sub -district there are some low hills with a slope between 15%-25%. The hilly and wavy morphology covers Rajagaluh and Sukahaji sub-districts in the south, Maja sub-district , part of Majalengka sub -district . The slope of the land in this area ranges from 15-40%, with an altitude of 300-700 m above sea level. The morphology of steep hills covers the area around Mount Ciremai , a small part of the Districts of Rajagaluh , Argapura , Sindang , Talaga , part of the Districts of Sindangwangi , Cingambul , Banjaran , Bantarujeg , Malausma and Lemahsugih and the northern part of Cikijing District. The slope in this area ranges from 25%-40% with an altitude between 400-2000 m above sea level.

Demographics

The population of Majalengka Regency Based on the BPS of Majalengka Regency in 2013 was 1,180,774 people consisting of 590,038 male residents and 590,736 female residents. The average population density of Majalengka Regency in 2013 was 981 people/km². The highest density is in Jatiwangi District with a density of 2,087 people/km².

The majority of the Majalengka people are of Sundanese ethnicity . The language used is Sundanese , but has several different meanings and vocabulary with Sundanese in the Priangan Region. Sundanese in Majalengka is a Middle Eastern dialect of Sundanese. In some areas of Majalengka the people are Cirebon people and use the Cirebon language , such as in the north and east of Jati Tujuh, Kertajati , Ligung , Sumberjaya and Patuanan Villages in Leuwimunding District .
